Getting There
Drive & Park Strategy
🚗 June 21 Drive
Depart Fort Wayne by 6–7 AM.
Route: I-69 → I-80 East through Ohio/Pennsylvania → Garden State Parkway → Exit 131/132 (Metropark).

~640 miles, 10–11 hours drive time. Aim to arrive Metropark by 3–4 PM, giving time to park, board a train, and reach hotel before dinner.
🚉 Metropark Station — Iselin, NJ
Best park-and-ride in NJ for NYC visitors.
Covered multi-story garage, 24 hrs, ~$10–11/day. ~45 min to NYC Penn Station/Moynihan Train Hall via NJ Transit Northeast Corridor Line.

Do before you go: Download the NexPass app — registers your plate so you just drive in/out. No ticket to lose. Confirm current rates at nexusparkingsystems.com.
🗽 Getting Around NYC
All core Midtown stops are walkable. Use Uber for the SoHo/Canal Street day (~25–30 min south of the hotel) and anything after 10 PM. Avoid the subway late at night; Uber is the right call for two women solo after dark. Budget $15–25 per Uber trip within Midtown; $20–30 for the SoHo run.
🚆 NJ Transit — How to Ride
Line: Northeast Corridor Line — direct, no transfers, Metropark → NYC Penn Station/Moynihan Hall (~43 min avg).

Frequency: Every 20 minutes. First train 12:05 AM, last from Metropark 11:17 PM. They will never be stranded.

Fare: ~$7–16/person one-way (peak vs. off-peak). Budget ~$15/person each way.

Buy tickets: Download the free NJ Transit app before leaving Fort Wayne. Create an account. Purchase through MyTix in the app — displays on phone, no paper needed. Select: Northeast Corridor → Metropark → New York Penn Station (or reverse). Don't activate the ticket until boarding.

Critical: Amtrak trains share the same tracks — NJ Transit tickets are NOT valid on Amtrak. The app's DepartureVision screen shows which departing trains are NJ Transit vs. Amtrak. Always board the NJ Transit train.
🔙 June 24 Return
Check out, Uber to Penn Station, NJ Transit back to Metropark. Aim to be at Penn Station by 11 AM to clear NJ before afternoon traffic builds. Back in Fort Wayne by 9–10 PM. Avoid departing after noon — NJ/PA weekend traffic is brutal by 1 PM.

Peace of Mind
Safety Notes
🏙️ Neighborhood Reality
The entire itinerary — Times Square, 5th Ave, Rockefeller Center, Central Park, Herald Square, Grand Central, and SoHo — covers some of the most heavily policed and tourist-trafficked areas in the world. Their hotel on 51st & 6th is in the heart of this corridor. Extremely safe during the day and evening. No concerns for this itinerary as planned.
🌙 After Dark
Times Square and Midtown are well-lit and busy until midnight. Stick to main streets after 10 PM. Use Uber for any return trip from SoHo/downtown and anything after 10 PM — don't walk 30 blocks at night.
🚕 Transportation
Use Uber or Lyft exclusively. Avoid hailing random cabs. The app shows driver name, plate, and route — much safer. Share trip status with Jon from the Uber app so he can track in real time.
📱 Stay Connected
Both should have Find My (iOS) location sharing active with Jon. Keep phones charged — bring a portable battery. Screenshot hotel address and Metropark station address offline in case of connectivity issues.
